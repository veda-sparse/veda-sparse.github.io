Component 01

Distilled Tile Scoring

Reconstruct max-pooled full-attention scores via triplet pooling (avg·max·min) + a KL distillation loss. Tile selection becomes an explicit, supervised target.

Component 02

Head-Aware Tiling

Per-head tile shape (pt, ph, pw) picked by offline search minimizing reconstruction error — spatial vs. temporal heads tiled differently.

Component 03

Tile-Skipping Kernel

Hopper kernel (ThunderKittens): TMA + warp specialization gathers only selected tiles, hiding memory latency behind tensor-core math. ~80% of FA3 MFU.

Analysis · Where the idea comes from

What makes a good sparse model?

What actually breaks sparse attention at high sparsity — and how Veda fixes it.

01 · The symptom

Structural artifacts emerge at high sparsity

Push sparsity past ~90% and prior methods don't merely lose detail — they develop a distinct class of structural artifacts: water-ripple warping that swims across surfaces, local geometric distortion, and temporal flicker between frames. These are qualitatively different from a dense model's semantic mistakes, and they are exactly what make naïve sparsity unusable for video.

02 · The cause

Mask quality — not the sparsity ratio — decides

We isolate the cause with a controlled study: at one fixed 90% budget we change only the mask. An Oracle mask — the true Top-k tiles read from full attention — preserves fidelity, while an average-pooling estimate collapses. So the bottleneck is not the sparsity ratio but how well the mask matches the tile-wise geometry of full attention. A simple tile-recall metric makes this concrete — Veda recovers 66.4% of the oracle's tiles vs. average pooling's 34.2%, and higher recall tracks artifact-free output.

03 · Heads are heterogeneous

One tiling can't fit all heads

Attention heads in video DiTs are strongly heterogeneous: some capture local spatial structure, others model long-range temporal dependencies — and the pattern drifts across diffusion timesteps. A single uniform tiling therefore under-aggregates some heads and over-aggregates others, eroding tile recall exactly where it matters.

The fix · head-aware tiling

Adapt the tile shape per head

  • Static tilings regress; head-aware is positive on every axis.
  • +9.5% Overall · +7.2% Motion vs. best static [4,4,8].
Why It Works · Theory

The derivation, step by step

A complete, self-contained argument for why distillation — not heavier pruning — is the right lever.

1
Energy-based view · why errors explode

Mask errors are amplified, not averaged

Self-attention is an energy-based model: the softmax over logits uj = q·kj is the gradient of a Log-Sum-Exp potential Ψ(u)=τ·log Σ exp(uj/τ), which is dominated by its largest term. If a sparse mask drops the dominant key, the surviving mass is renormalized — every kept probability is multiplied by Z/Ẑ > 1, so confidence is inflated and re-assigned to noise. Because softmax is non-linear, the error is amplified, not averaged away — a structural hallucination. Faithfully recovering the oracle Top-k is therefore a prerequisite for stability at high sparsity, not a refinement.

Ψ(u)=τ·log Σj exp(uj/τ)  ⟹  P̃j = (Z / Ẑ) · Pj > Pj
↓ so we must estimate the Q·K ranking accurately — and cheaply
2
Sufficient statistics · why a mean is not enough

Triplet pooling reconstructs the dot product

A tile score must be estimated from a few pooled statistics. A single mean (average pooling) is provably insufficient: the dot product decomposes into a centroid term plus a covariance term, and pooling to the mean discards the covariance — mean-similar tiles become indistinguishable and the score matrix loses rank. We restore the missing second-order term with extrema: by the Bhatia–Davis inequality the variance of a bounded variable obeys σ² ≤ (M−μ)(μ−m), so the triplet {avg, max, min} is a bounding box that pins down each tile's spread — enough to reconstruct the score at negligible extra I/O.

q·k = d·μqμk + d·Cov(q,k)  ·  σ² ≤ (M−μ)(μ−m)
↓ but this bound is only tight when a tile's tokens are similar
3
Bandwidth minimization · why tiling must adapt

Head-aware tiling minimizes intra-tile variance

The neglected residual interaction inside a tile is bounded (Cauchy–Schwarz) by E ≤ B·σq·σk, so the estimate degrades as intra-tile variance grows — a tile's tokens must be similar. Under 3D RoPE, tokens lie on a spatio-temporal manifold where affinity decays with geodesic distance; a naïve raster tiling scatters temporal neighbours and inflates variance. Head-Aware Tiling searches per-head factorizations (pt, ph, pw) that fold the manifold into memory while keeping high-affinity tokens together — minimizing attention bandwidth, and with it the variance that Step 2 depends on. The ablation above confirms the gain on every quality axis.

E = |Σi ⟨rqi, rki⟩| ≤ B · σq · σk

Putting it together

Distill the oracle ranking (Step 1) using sufficient statistics (Step 2) under variance-minimizing, head-aware tilings (Step 3). Each piece is necessary — together they keep the tile mask aligned with full attention even at 95% sparsity.
